Design Philosophy: Inspired by Royalty

Homey Design embraces a rich design philosophy rooted in classical European traditions — particularly the baroque and rococo periods. With every piece, there's an unmistakable blend of artistic heritage and modern-day craftsmanship.

The brand’s vision is to bring regal aesthetics into contemporary homes without compromising functionality. Ornate carvings, elaborate scrollwork, button-tufted upholstery, and lavish fabrics like velvet and jacquard define its collections. Gold and silver finishes, intricate inlays, and marble accents contribute to the brand's unmistakable grandeur.

This design language allows Homey Design furniture to serve as both functional pieces and works of art, transforming ordinary interiors into majestic living spaces.

Craftsmanship: A Labor of Art and Precision

Each Homey Design piece is a result of meticulous craftsmanship. Skilled artisans hand-carve the elaborate details from solid hardwoods like poplar, birch, and mahogany. The wood is kiln-dried to ensure durability and resistance to warping, then shaped and finished by hand.

Upholstery is just as refined. Homey Design uses high-density foam, spring systems, and rich textiles that combine comfort with longevity. Decorative trimmings, fringe tassels, nailhead accents, and hand-applied gold leafing are used liberally to amplify the sense of drama and luxury.

The result? Furniture that lasts generations and remains visually captivating throughout the years.

History and Origins of Homey Design

While Homey Design is recognized today as a premier luxury furniture brand, its roots trace back to the late 20th century in Southern California. The company began as a small family-owned business with a passion for ornate furniture reminiscent of European palaces. Over time, thanks to an unwavering dedication to quality and beauty, the brand gained national — and eventually global — recognition.

From early beginnings crafting hand-carved sofas and chairs, Homey Design rapidly evolved by embracing advanced manufacturing techniques while retaining its old-world artistry. Today, it represents a fusion of heritage and innovation, with products beloved by interior designers, luxury developers, and discerning homeowners.

Materials Used: The Backbone of Quality

One of the key aspects that sets Homey Design apart is its commitment to premium materials. These include:

  • Solid hardwood frames (poplar, mahogany, birch)

  • High-density foam and sinuous spring support

  • Hand-applied veneers

  • Rich jacquard fabrics, silk blends, velvet, chenille

  • Gold and silver foil accents

  • Marble, onyx, and inlaid glass for tabletops

The combination of these luxury-grade materials ensures not only a visually stunning appearance but also long-term durability, making each piece suitable for daily use in high-end homes.

Signature Design Elements to Know

If you’re shopping or styling with Homey Design, here are signature traits that define its collections:

  • Scrolled wood arms with baroque carvings

  • Tufted backs with crystal or covered buttons

  • Metallic finishes in gold, antique bronze, or silver patina

  • Floral and damask upholstery with fringe

  • Oversized silhouettes meant to dominate a room

  • Contrasting throw pillows with embroidered patterns or tassels

These visual cues give Homey Design furniture an identity unlike any other — immediately recognizable and unmistakably regal.

Caring for Your Homey Design Investment

To keep your Homey Design pieces pristine:

  • Use non-abrasive cloths to clean wood and metallic finishes

  • Avoid placing items in direct sunlight, which can fade upholstery

  • Use a vacuum with a brush attachment for fabric maintenance

  • Re-apply gold or silver leaf finishes via professional refinishers when needed

  • Use furniture sliders when moving pieces — due to their heavy, solid wood construction
